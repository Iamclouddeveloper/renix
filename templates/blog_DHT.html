Derby Hindu Temple (Geeta Bhavan)

Welcome to the official website of Derby Hindu Temple (Geeta Bhavan), a sacred place of devotion, community, and cultural heritage in the heart of Derby. The temple serves as a spiritual and cultural sanctuary for devotees from all walks of life, upholding the values and teachings of Sanatana Dharma.

About the Temple

Established in the early 1980s, the Derby Hindu Temple has grown into a vibrant religious and cultural hub for the Hindu community in Derbyshire and surrounding areas. The temple is home to beautifully adorned deities including Lord Rama, Sita, Lakshmana, Hanuman, Lord Krishna, Radha, and Lord Shiva.

The temple is a space for prayer, meditation, celebration, and education. It is managed by a dedicated committee and volunteers who ensure the temple's smooth operations, events, and community outreach programs.

Regular Services and Activities

  • Daily Aarti: Morning and evening prayers with bhajans and chanting.
  • Weekly Satsangs: Spiritual gatherings with kirtans, discourses, and prasadam.
  • Festivals: Grand celebrations of Diwali, Navratri, Janmashtami, Maha Shivratri, Holi, and more.
  • Children’s Classes: Moral education, Hindu history, language, and bhajan singing sessions.
  • Yoga and Meditation: Periodic wellness sessions promoting inner peace and well-being.

Community Outreach

The Derby Hindu Temple plays an active role in supporting the wider community through interfaith dialogues, charity drives, senior support programs, and educational workshops. It fosters unity, compassion, and understanding while encouraging the next generation to take pride in their cultural identity.

Temple Hall & Facilities

The temple includes a main prayer hall, cultural activity rooms, kitchen facilities, and event spaces that can be hired for religious ceremonies, weddings, and cultural functions. We welcome everyone to participate and make use of these sacred facilities.
